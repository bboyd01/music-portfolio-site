About Me

Background

Hello! I'm Brett Boyd, and I compose music for various types of media. I'm currently a college student at Lipscomb University, and I'm studying Music Composition and Web Development (fun fact: I coded this website from scratch).

My composition journey begins with my humble musical roots; music does not run in my family. In fact, my family background is more "rednecks and farmers" than "musicians and creators." Despite that, I've lived my entire life with a deep, visceral fascination with music, with its enchanting sounds, moving melodies, and mesmerizing textures. My childhood was full of music from things like Star Wars (on the old VHS tapes!), Super Mario Galaxy, and Wii Sports. Years and years later, I realize just how foundational those early musical experiences were, laying the groundwork for my future music-making endeavors.

Throughout my school years, I progressed from learning the recorder in elementary school to joining our concert band in middle school, and I dabbled in guitar and audio production during high school. All the while, I consistently put time into an engaging side hobby‒ music composition. After the painfully dreadful (albeit imaginative) bits of music I made in middle school, my work would consistently grow more coherent, complex, and creative, fueled by my curiosity and strong sense of intuition. By my last year of high school, I had written the score for a documentary project and done a few freelance projects.

My time as a college student so far has been full of inspiring experiences and enriching musical opportunities. I've been able to study music in broader contexts, enroll in regular choir rehearsals and piano classes, and‒ most importantly‒ music composition lessons, where I've grown so much as a music maker. With every project under my belt, I've honed my skills and challenged myself to improve. Although I'm lightyears ahead of where I began, I still take inspiration from the music I revered as a kid‒ Wii games and Star Wars movies still get a lot of love from me.

What sets me apart?

Music isn't just something I create; it's something I understand. I've studied the music in everything from YouTube ads to animated films to NES games to help answer the question of "What music works here?" Rather than using a one-size-fits-all approach to composing, I meticulously evaluate the musical needs of every project, with every project sounding different. I know the distinct "norms" for different media types like films and videogames (but as they say, you have to know the rules to break them). Everyone wants a score that "sounds good," but I deliver music that is full of intentionality and purpose, music that exists for a reason‒ to help you tell your story.

Check out my projects to get a feel for the way I compose. Words can only describe music so much...
