Adaptive Game Score

How would the music in a Star Wars game sound if it weren't so... Star Wars-sounding? In this project, I replaced the original music in a piece of gameplay footage with a custom score-- in my own style. Besides giving this project a fresh, synthy sci-fi score, I created multiple audio layers that dynamically respond to the player's actions. Finally, I put together a mockup of this system for the demo that you see here!

A world of sound, from scratch

As part of a summer project, I developed a small 8-bit videogame as a personal project. A large chunk of that development time went to the music and sound design, which I created entirely with simple synth patches. The levels resound with music that feels both energetic and emotive. The crisp sound effects mesh perfectly with the art assets and music score-- bleeps and bloops definitely have their place in the gaming world! And making those bleeps and bloops sound coherent and artistic was an extremely engaging challenge.

Full Orchestra Theme

We all know that "less is more," but sometimes "more is more." The expressive power of an orchestra is tough to beat, and I love making use of all the varied tone colors and musical textures that an orchestra offers. The audio example here fits the "final castle level" trope, with a very final and climactic sound-- all mixed and mastered to give the track a polished, professional sound. If you need a score that sounds "big," then I'd love to get in touch!
