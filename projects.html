Recent Projects

Here are a few examples of my recent work, including audio demos and project descriptions.

Full Orchestra Piece

Brett Boyd · Inside the Walls

Even with the sheer number of possibilities with an orchestra, I still stick to my roots in minimalism and simplicity; I pay just as much attention to detail and polish for twenty instruments as I do for just one. This somewhat sullen yet climactic piece tugs the player into the action, reinforcing the gravity of their story. The same musical idea is repeated, tossed around, developed, and recontextualized to create a fresh sense of atmosphere that rises and falls‒ a perfect underscore for the final struggle of any adventurous story.

8-Bit Theme

Brett Boyd · 8-bit Theme

Staying true to the limitations of hardware like the NES, true 8-bit music typically only allows two square pulse waves, a triangle wave, and a noise channel. However, the challenge of following these limitations fosters creativity and ingenuity, forcing composers to make every note count. By creating music for both a title screen and a level, I pushed myself to make big things out of small ideas‒ shaping and developing just one musical theme, just like the legendary game composers of the 8-bit era.

Adaptive Game Score

Brett Boyd · Star Wars KOTOR Rescore - No SFX

How would the music in a Star Wars game sound if it weren't so... Star Wars-sounding? In this project, I replaced the original music in a section of gameplay footage with a custom score‒ in my own style. Besides giving this project a fresh, synthy sci-fi sound, I created multiple audio layers that dynamically respond to the player's actions. Finally, I put together a mockup of this system for the demo that you see here!
